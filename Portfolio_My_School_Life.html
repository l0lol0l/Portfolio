I- My School Life Prior to Departure

Until August 2021, I adhered to the French curriculum within the confines of a traditional classroom setting. My four years of “collège” (middle school + one year of high school) were spent at “Collège Saint-Yves” in Tréguier, in Brittany, France. At the end of this period, I sat for the DNB (Diplôme National du Brevet) exam, which is equivalent to the GCSE (General Certificate of Secondary Education), and I achieved honors in this examination.

During those four years, my coursework encompassed a wide range of subjects including mathematics, chemistry, physics, biology, French, English, Spanish, history, geography, civics, physical education, music, and art. Additionally, starting from the second year, I began studying Latin, an optional subject. I consistently ranked among the top students in my class.


II- My School Life until French Baccalauréat

In August 2021, my family and I embarked on a sailing journey around the world, leaving behind the traditional classroom setting. Faced with the challenge of continuing my education while sailing the high seas, I looked for alternative options. Through collaboration with a private school called “Cours Pi”, I transitioned to a homeschooling approach that allowed me to remain aligned with the French curriculum, although with some adjustments.

Over the course of two school years, I continued studying mathematics, chemistry, physics, biology, French, English, history, geography, and civics. Additionally, I opted for three elective courses: advanced math, engineering sciences, and computer sciences. However, due to the unique circumstances of our travels, we decided to forgo formal instruction in music and art.

Despite the unconventional nature of my learning environment, I remained constant in my academic pursuits. In preparation for the “épreuves anticipées de français du baccalauréat” (anticipated French baccalauréat exams), I dedicated myself to rigorous study and preparation. These exams, which correspond to the language component of the high school diploma, presented a significant challenge, particularly given the remote location where I took them… Tahiti.

I am proud to share that my efforts produced honorable results. In the oral component of the exams, I achieved a score of 16/20, reflecting my proficiency in spoken French and my ability to articulate ideas effectively. While the written portion posed greater difficulty, I attained a score of 10/20, showcasing my determination to overcome obstacles and perform to the best of my ability under unconventional circumstances.


III- Current School Life

After two years of sailing around the world while following the French curriculum, I made the decision to transfer to Clonlara School. This institution operates under the US educational system, utilizing credits and culminating in a high school diploma. One notable advantage of Clonlara School, aside from its provision for homeschooling, lies in its flexibility regarding elective courses.

Consequently, I had the opportunity to delve into subjects such as public speaking, sailing yacht design, and a course titled "English for STEM." Additionally, on top of the proficiency level in English I acquired while sailing around the world, the school also recognized my efforts in obtaining my boating license and acquiring proficiency in HTML/CSS for the development of this website.

During this final year of high school, I actively worked towards obtaining my high school diploma and continue to strive towards this objective.
