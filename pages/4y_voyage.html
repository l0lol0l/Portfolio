The 4 Year Voyage

Our journey began on the 4th of August 2021 in the small French town of Tréguier. From there, we sailed along the Atlantic coast before crossing the Atlantic Ocean. After exploring the Caribbean, we transited the Panama Canal and continued on to discover the Pacific Ocean and its beautiful islands. I have deliberately used the word “we”, as this journey was not a solitary one. I am referring to my family —my parents and my younger brother— with whom I shared this extraordinary adventure aboard our sailing vessel, Milonga, a 47 foot aluminium monohull. After almost four years of travelling, we eventually arrived in New Zealand, where I disembarked to begin my maritime studies. Throughout this voyage, I learned a variety of skills and life lessons that continue to shape my perspective to this day. From the moment we set sail, every day presented new challenges and opportunities for growth.

First and foremost, navigating the vast ocean taught me the importance of adaptability and resourcefulness. Weather conditions are ever-changing, and we had to constantly adjust our sails and course to stay on track. Through trial and error, I sharpened my ability to make quick decisions and improvise solutions, skills that have proven invaluable in both my personal and professional life.

Moreover, the self-sufficiency required on a sailing voyage strengthened a sense of independence and resourcefulness within me. From repairing equipment to navigating unfamiliar waters, I discovered the satisfaction that comes from relying on my own skills and ingenuity. This newfound confidence has empowered me to tackle challenges in other areas of my life with determination.

Another lesson I learned from the simplicity of life at sea, is to appreciate simple things. Away from the distractions of modern life, I relearned to appreciate simple things like the rhythm of the waves or the majesty of the starlit sky. It was a humbling reminder of our place in the vast universe.

Living in close quarters with my family also taught me the significance of patience and understanding. Conflicts were inevitable, but through open communication and empathy, we were able to resolve disagreements. Learning to appreciate each other's strengths and weaknesses made us closer to each other.

Furthermore, exploring diverse countries and cultures along our sailing journey has been a very enriching experience. From the bustling streets of Colombia to the serene beaches of Fiji, each place teaches us something new about how diverse our world is. Immersing myself in new languages, cultures, and traditions has helped me see things from different perspectives and understand how we're all connected. Meeting locals and other travelers has taught me the importance of empathy and getting along with people, regardless of geographical origin. These cultural exchanges have not only expanded my worldview but also instilled in me a profound respect for the beauty of diversity.

Additionally, meticulous management of energy and resources was imperative during our voyage. The key resources included fuel, electricity, fresh water, and food. Utilizing a wind generator, a hydrogenerator and solar panels, we achieved significant autonomy in electricity, while a desalination system ensured our freshwater needs were met. Through careful regulation and conservation, we maintained self-sufficiency. However, replenishing fuel and food stocks remained a regular requirement. These practices instilled in me a heightened awareness of our environmental footprint and the necessity of responsible consumption.

In conclusion, the myriad experiences of our nautical trip have sculpted me into a more resilient, adaptable, and appreciative individual. Each challenge surmounted, each lesson learned has imbued me with a deeper understanding of myself and the world around me, enriching my journey in ways I could never have imagined.
